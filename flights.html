Earth ORBIT

Many astronauts have remarked that one experience more incredible than looking out into space is to look down on our own home planet. From being so far from ground you’ll gain an appreciation of how small our world is. Being able to see the planet in a single view will take your breath away; from the snow capped tips of the poles, deepest of blues of our oceans, the lush green landscapes and yellow scorched sands of the hottest deserts overlaid with the ever changing swirls created by the global jet streams – the colours of which are amplified against the backdrop of deep dark space. each flight is guaranteed to provide two orbits of the Earth. The pre-determined trajectory path crosses the globe in a seemingly diagonal pattern ensuring visibility across all continents – passing over Earth’s equator up-to 4 times! Every flight also includes ‘Astro:Zero’ a unique opportunity to unshackle the harness and experience zero gravity. During this time you’ll be free to choose how you wish to mark the moment, whether that’s participating with the on-board Astro:Science team’s latest research, part-taking in a game of Zero:Ball or simply living out a child hood dream of ‘flying’. The onboard meal served at this time is also sure to become the most memorable meal of your life!

Lunar:ORBIT

The influence the Moon has on life on Earth cannot be over-stated. The gravitational pull being a major factor to Earth’s tidal patterns; nature's original answer to GPS for migrating animals or simply acting as a torch in the night-sky for nocturnal activities. This is your chance to not only get up and close with our natural satellite but also see something the strongest telescopes on Earth never will…… what is known as – the dark side of the moon. You’ll also be guaranteed a solar eclipse – with no clouds!

Lunar:Landing

For those with the strongest of passions to experience space life! You’ll accompany an Astro:Corp crew on a scheduled journey to Lunar Base 5. Our team will customise your trip to the Moon to your unique requirements as you explore the lunar surface; traverse the surface in a lunar buggy? a visit to Tranquility Base (the site of Apollo 11 landing to see the footprints from Buzz Aldrin and Neil Armstrong’s visit)? A game of zero-gravity golf? Or an excursion on our new Astro:Explorer to the ‘dark side of the moon’ (don’t worry, it’s not permanently dark there!). There’s also the chance to extend with a stay at our Lunar Base 5.

Space:Safari

The ultimate experience, combining all of the above with an onward journey to Astro:View (our solar observatory). This is strictly a 3 month stay (due to health regulations and advance scheduling), but this will seem too short as you immerse yourself with the deep space experience. You’ll be invited to join the scientific team which will see you; observe our planetary neighbours, measure radiation waves from far flung galaxies, sending (and listening for a reply!) to your own voice beamed out beyond our solar system, determine the path and take command of the launch of your own pathfinder probe (providing a lifetime direct view from space), as well as witness many of the thousands of cosmic displays from passing comets and astounding solar light shows. You’ll also continue training onboard the station with the aim of performing a space walk! If you’re lucky, you may also get the chance to meet with one of our crews bound for Mars who will be willing to take a personal time-capsule from you to place on the Red Planet!

The future’s bright, the future’s Mars!

Astro:Corp plans for manned flights to Mars are long established. The first of a series of unmanned flights have been in operation since early 2020. These initial flights have served a number of purposes, from conducting sample tests on the environment, providing real time weather reports and conducting explorations in search of minerals, water or signs of microbial or even historic life. But in addition to furthering our understanding of Mars, each flight has successful delivered components of the Mars outpost (Camp Primus). Through a combination of automation / pre-programmed machinery and remote controlled robotics (principally operated from the team on Lunar 4 Base 5) a series of habitable domes have been constructed. Furthermore, flora is successfully being grown in oxygen sealed units. These have paved the way for the first of a series of our manned flights which are on-track to depart Earth by late 2022. Whilst the first few years will be exclusively dedicated to further our understanding of the red planet, plans are on schedule to activate our Mars tourism route by 2025.
